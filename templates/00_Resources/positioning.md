# Pendo platform positioning

*Pendo's current platform thesis and C-suite plays. Read alongside `operating-model.md` (deal mechanics frame). This file is the positioning content COTM is built on, not the conversation methodology itself.*

---

## Platform thesis: 5-lead context layer

Pendo reads five signals in concert. Competitors read one or two.

| Signal | What it captures |
| --- | --- |
| **Do** | What users do in the product |
| **Say** | What users say in surveys, NPS, comments |
| **Feel** | How users feel (sentiment, friction signals) |
| **See** | What users see (session replay) |
| **Converse** | What users converse with agents about |

Competitive position:
- Amplitude, Mixpanel, Qualtrics: 1-lead
- Fullstory: 2-lead
- Pendo: 5-lead

Differentiation line: *"A model trained on one signal will make confident wrong decisions."*

## Earned-right narrative

The bottoms-up enterprise playbook. Box, DocuSign, and Smartsheet ran the same motion:

1. **Land bottoms-up.** Product managers, ICs, business-line leaders.
2. **Build the footprint.** Use cases, usage data, success stories, named champions.
3. **Earn the C-suite.** Walk in with proof, not a pitch.

## Five-verb model

The "best teams do" frame for product-led organisations:

**Observe > Understand > Act > Prove > Optimise.**

Each verb maps to a Pendo capability and a customer outcome. Used as the spine of the Pendo overview and the CPO pitch.

---

## The CIO pitch

> *Pendo is the control plane for your agentic transformation: the only system that sees both your existing software and your agent portfolio, drives the adoption that makes both work, and gives you the data, for your CFO, to recognise when the bet has paid off.*

**Before scenario.** Flying blind on a CFO-mandated transformation. Dozens or hundreds of parallel AI experiments. No shared definition of what is working. AI budget is additive, not yet replacing anything. The CFO will ask, soon, when it pays back. There is no answer today.

**Five-step value chain.** Discovery > Evaluation > Adoption > Context > Inflection Points.

**Monday-morning question.** *"When the CFO asks you what the AI investment has produced, what do you show them?"*

Most CIOs cannot answer this cleanly today.

---

## The CPO pitch

> *Pendo is the context layer to win the battle for your users: the view that closes the loop from observation to optimisation, makes the transition in your hybrid product natural, gives you the evidence to inform your investments, and helps your team transform fast enough to win.*

**Five jobs Pendo does end-to-end.** Observe > Understand > Act > Prove > Optimise.

**Anxiety hook.** The CPO's deepest anxiety is not the tools. It is whether their team can transform fast enough to use them. Reference: Satya Nadella, Davos January 2026, "Full Stack Builder organisation".

**Monday-morning question.** *"How well does your AI agent actually understand the people using it?"*

Most CPOs feel the answer is thin as they say it.

---

## Expansion thesis

Land one side, unlock the other.

Customer context (the data the CPO collects) is the fuel for every internal AI agent the CIO is building. The sales agent should know what the customer does in the product before pitching upsell. Pendo, sitting on both sides with A2A, MCP, and Data Sync, is the only system positioned to deliver that context.
