How this dashboard is calculated

A read on the quarter, not a forecast

This view sits on top of live Salesforce opportunity data scoped to the people on your team. It does not change anything in Salesforce. It is a working surface for weekly pipeline calls: a weighted math call from the forecast categories your reps have set, an educated-edge call that pulls back on deals carrying multiple risk signals, and a copyable plain-text summary you can paste into Slack or email.

Weighted math call

Floor 100% + Forecast 80% + Upside 25%

Each open opportunity with a close date inside the current fiscal quarter is bucketed by its Salesforce Sales_Forecast_Category__c value, then weighted:

CategoryWeightEligibilityRationale
Commit 100% All commit deals Counted at face value. This is the floor.
Forecast 80% All forecast deals Discounts the optimism bias most reps carry in this category.
Upside 25% {{METHODOLOGY_UPSIDE_FLOOR}} Smaller upside deals are excluded to avoid noise. Larger ones get a token weight. 
Math call = Σ(Commit × 1.0) + Σ(Forecast × 0.8) + Σ(Upside ≥ team floor × 0.25)
Educated edge call

Math call minus a 50% haircut on flagged deals

Any deal carrying two or more risk flags (see below) has its weighted contribution cut in half. The resulting number is the edge call: a more conservative read that takes deal hygiene into account, not just the rep's category placement. 

Edge call = Math call - Σ(deals_with_2+_flags × weighted_contribution × 0.5)

The risk-adjustment line under the edge call value shows the size of this discount and how many deals contributed to it.

Risk flags

Four signals applied per deal

FlagTriggerWhat it usually means
Pushed Nx PushCount ≥ 5 Close date has slipped five or more times. Strong signal of a stalled deal.
Closes in Nd CloseDate within next 30 days Near-term deals carry the most forecast risk. Surfaces them for attention.
MEDDPICC N/8 Four or more MEDDPICC fields empty Qualification gap. Hard to call confidence on deals with thin discovery.
No next steps Next_Steps__c and NextStep both blank or under 5 characters No documented forward motion. Often a coaching opportunity in the 1:1.

A deal counts toward the edge-call haircut only when it carries two or more of the four flags. Single-flag deals are surfaced in the table but do not change the math.

Scope and data source
Pipeline scopeOpen opportunities owned by reps in your team configuration, with a close date inside the current fiscal quarter.
Team configurationRead from System/team.yaml in RVP mode, or System/avp-teams.yaml in AVP mode. Skill prompts on first run to surface which mode you are in.
Fiscal calendarQuarter labels computed at runtime from fiscal_year_start_month in System/user-profile.yaml. Pendo default is February.
Refresh behaviourThe artifact re-queries Salesforce every time it loads. There is no cached data: closing and reopening the artifact gives you the latest numbers.
Source of truthSalesforce. This dashboard reads only; nothing in Salesforce is changed by viewing or copying from it.
What this is not
  • Not the official forecast. The official call goes through your manager's process; this is your working view.
  • Not predictive. The math is descriptive: it takes what is in Salesforce today and weights it.
  • Not a coaching report. The risk flags surface conversation prompts, not performance judgements.
  • Not a substitute for talking to the rep. Use this to focus the 1:1, then test the data with the human in the room.
