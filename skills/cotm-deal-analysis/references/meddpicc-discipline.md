# MEDDPICC Discipline - Reference for the Brief

The COTM Lab Day 2 framing: **MEDDPICC isn't a form. It's a diagnostic.** Every field can be answered as Know or Assume. The brief is graded on whether the rep can defend each cell against that test.

---

## The question each letter answers

| Letter | Field | The question that surfaces what's real |
| --- | --- | --- |
| M | Metrics | What does success look like in numbers? Has the buyer agreed - or did the rep propose it and the buyer not push back? |
| E | Economic Buyer | Have you spoken to the EB directly, not through the champion? What did they say about business impact? |
| D | Decision Criteria | Does the customer have formal evaluation criteria, or is this still exploratory? Are the criteria shaped to favour Pendo's differentiators? |
| D | Decision Process | Can you map every step AND every name in the approval chain? Specific names, not "leadership". |
| P | Paper Process | Is procurement engaged? Has legal started? Is there an actual timeline, or just a desired close date? |
| I | Identified Pain | Is the pain specific, quantified, in the customer's words - or is it the rep's summary? |
| C | Champion | Has your champion stuck their neck out for you? What have they done when you weren't in the room? |
| C | Competition | What's the real reason they'd choose the competitor? What does your champion tell you when they're being honest? |

If any answer starts with "I think" or "probably", that's not a Know. Mark it ⚠️ or ❌ accordingly.

---

## Status icons - calibration

`✅` **Know** - backed by direct evidence in transcripts or in SFDC narrative fields. Quotable, attributable, dated.

`⚠️` **Partial** - some evidence exists but a meaningful gap remains. Examples:
- Pain identified but not quantified.
- EB named but no direct conversation.
- Champion engaged but hasn't acted independently.
- Decision process partly mapped but missing approval threshold.

`❌` **Assume / Blank** - no evidence, or only the rep's hypothesis. Examples:
- Metrics proposed by the rep, customer never confirmed.
- Decision criteria assumed from analogous deals.
- Competition listed as "none" with no transcript evidence either way.

**Calibration check:** Stage 0-2 deals typically have 1-2 ✅ at most. If you're writing a Stage 1 brief with seven ✅, you're confirming hypotheses instead of testing them. Read the transcripts again and ask: did the customer actually *say* this, or did the rep say it and they didn't push back?

## Champion vs Coach vs Fan - the most common miscall

This distinction is the single most common error in early-stage briefs. From the COTM Lab Day 2 deck:

| Type | Behaviour | Mark as |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Champion** | Articulates the problem in their own words. Advocates internally when rep isn't there. Creates access to power. Has personal stake in the outcome. | C ✅ |
| **Coach** | Shares information. Responsive. Engaged. Likes the solution. Does NOT push it forward when not asked. | C ⚠️ |
| **Fan** | Positive sentiment. Verbal agreement. Won't take internal risk. Credibility not on the line. | C ❌ |
| **Guide** | Helps navigate the organisation. Not invested in the outcome. Provides intros when asked. | C ❌ |
| **Blocker** | Resists change. Prefers status quo. Influences others against you. | C ❌ + flag as risk |

**The Champion Test** (5 questions - all must be yes for ✅):
1. If you're not in the room, would they advocate for this initiative?
2. Have they taken specific action internally on your behalf?
3. Can they clearly explain the business problem and impact?
4. Have they helped you gain access to the Economic Buyer?
5. If this deal depended entirely on them, would it move forward?

If 1-2 are no, mark Coach (⚠️). If 3+ are no, mark Fan or Guide (❌).

## Economic Buyer vs Authorizer vs Influencer

| Type | Behaviour | Mark as |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Economic Buyer** | Approves budget without escalation. Owns the Required Business Outcomes. Engages on business impact. Can kill the deal unilaterally. | E ✅ (only if rep has spoken with them directly) |
| **Authorizer** | Signs the contract. Needs others' buy-in to act. Engages late, on terms only. | E ⚠️ |
| **Influencer** | Has a voice, not a vote. Recommends or blocks. Doesn't control budget. Often a department head. | E ❌ |

**Common error:** marking E ✅ because the champion described someone as "the decision-maker". That's hearsay. ✅ requires the rep to have spoken with the EB directly.

**Sub-EB pattern:** in matrix orgs, a functional decision-maker may own the budget for *this category* even if the formal EB owns the wider P&L. Both should appear in the brief - the formal EB and the operational EB. Treat them as two separate access targets.

## Decision Process - what good looks like

A `✅` Decision Process answer reads like a map:

> Step 1: Champion (Peter) presents the recommendation to Michael Nufio (interim head, merchant experience) by end of week.
> Step 2: Michael reviews and either funds from his discretionary budget (under $100k) or escalates to the TPP committee.
> Step 3: TPP committee meets next on June 17. Submissions close June 10. If Pendo isn't in the submission, the deal moves to the next cycle (Q4).
> Step 4: TPP approval triggers procurement engagement. Procurement contact not yet identified.

If you can't write it this concretely, mark ⚠️ and add a Gap.

## Competition - what to look for

Three categories, all relevant:

1. **Direct competitors.** Mixpanel, Amplitude, Fullstory, Whatfix, WalkMe, Gainsight, etc. Look for: tool names mentioned, what they currently use, what they've evaluated.
2. **Internal build.** "We could build that ourselves." Common in engineering-heavy orgs. Look for: references to engineering capacity, data team plans, existing data warehouse projects.
3. **Do-nothing.** The status quo. Almost always present. Risk increases when: no compelling event, no quantified pain, no champion with personal stake.

If the brief doesn't name the competition, that absence is itself the failure mode. Write `❌` and add a Gap that says "Competition unknown - ask champion directly on next call".

---

## The meta-question

For any cell where the rep is tempted to write ✅, run this test before committing:

> "You said you know that. What would it take for you to be wrong?"

If the answer is "if the customer changed their mind" or "if I misheard them on a call" - that's still ✅.

If the answer is "if my read of [title / hierarchy / past behaviour] is wrong" - that's ⚠️ or ❌.

This is the discipline. The brief is only useful if it's honest.
