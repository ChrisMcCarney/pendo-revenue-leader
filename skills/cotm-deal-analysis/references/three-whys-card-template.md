# The 3 Whys Card - Companion Template

The 3 Whys card is the rep's **in-call artifact**. It is paired with the full COTM Deal Analysis brief: the brief is for prep, the card is for execution.

The format mirrors the COTM Lab Day 1 demo training. Reps already rehearse conversations around Why anything / Why now / Why Pendo. By giving them a deal-specific version of that frame, the card slots into the muscle memory they already have.

**Length target: 250-350 words.** This is the tight, screenshot-able, paste-into-Slack artifact. If a section is running long, cut.

---

```markdown
# {Account Name} - 3 Whys

*{DD Month YYYY} - {SFDC Stage} - Owner: {Owner Name} - Next call: {date}*

---

## Why anything?

{One tight paragraph (~60-80 words) that distils Current State into a single narrative. Lead with the most powerful customer quote - verbatim, attributed. Follow with the cost of doing nothing in one sentence. The rep should be able to deliver this from memory after reading it once.}

> *Example:* "Tyro is bleeding 82% of merchants on the getting-started form before submit ("I want to kill it with fire" - Peter, May 15). Mixpanel is paid for but operationally unusable - Peter, a Staff PM, doesn't have a login. Every in-app message is a "multi-team, multi-day shit fight". The cost: opportunity revenue on lending and partner-led onboarding, plus an unjustifiable Mixpanel renewal in July.*

---

## Why now?

{One tight paragraph (~40-60 words) naming the compelling event with a specific date or deadline. If there is no real compelling event, say so explicitly - that absence is itself the rep's headline talking point.}

> *Example:* "Tyro's TPP (Tyro Planning Period) closes for next-six-months funding in the next 3-4 weeks. Mixpanel contract expires in July. Partner-led onboarding launches late June - the natural POC surface. If Pendo doesn't make this TPP cycle, the deal moves to the next one and momentum dies."*

---

## Why Pendo?

{One tight paragraph (~60-80 words) naming the 1-2 lead differentiators that map to the customer's pain, with the close-trap question for each. Speak in the customer's language, not Pendo's.}

> *Example:* "Two angles. **Insight to action in one platform**: "If the lending launch had been a five-minute guide instead of a multi-team shit fight, how much earlier would it have hit your merchants?" **All-in-one with shared data foundation**: "When you look at the funnel from tyro.com through KYC into Merchant Portal and now into Thriday, how much does it matter that these stages live in three tools that don't speak the same language?""*

---

## The one move on next call

{A single sentence: who + specific question + date. The thing the AD will write on a sticky note before walking in. Drawn from the #1 entry in the full brief's Recommended Next Actions.}

> *Example:* "On 12 June reset demo, ask Peter: 'When you next brief Michael, would it help for me to join for 20 minutes to walk the business case and hear what he's measuring on the merchant experience patch?' Goal: direct EB conversation before TPP submission closes."*
```

---

## Notes on the card

**Why this format works for the AD.** The 3 Whys frame is the COTM Lab Day 1 demo training format - every AD already rehearses around it. By giving them a deal-specific version of the same frame, the card slots into existing muscle memory. They read the brief once for context, then carry the card mentally into the conversation.

**Why one paragraph per Why.** The card is meant to be glanceable in 30 seconds. Bullets fragment attention; a single tight paragraph reads as a narrative the rep can deliver. If a Why genuinely needs more, that's a signal the brief should be richer there - not the card.

**Why "the one move" sits alone.** The full brief has up to 5 Recommended Next Actions. The card has one. That forces the rep to pick the single highest-leverage move and rehearse for it. Five priorities is no priority.

**When to skip a Why.** If a Why genuinely cannot be written from the evidence - e.g. no compelling event has emerged ("Why now?") - write the card with the Why explicitly empty: "*No confirmed compelling event. This is the brief's biggest deal risk.*" That honesty is what makes the card useful instead of motivational.

**Companion formats.** If the user asks for a slide (.pptx), an HTML one-pager, or a Slack block, build it from this card's content. The 3 Whys + the one move is already the tight presentable form - different surfaces just re-render it.
